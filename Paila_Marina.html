Paila Marina

Paila marina is a delicious and hearty seafood soup that hails from the coastal regions of Chile. With its rich and savory broth, tender seafood, and aromatic herbs and spices, paila marina is a true taste of Chilean coastal cuisine. Its origins can be traced back to the fishing villages along the country's long and rugged coastline, where fishermen would prepare the soup with the freshest catch of the day. Today, paila marina remains a beloved dish throughout Chile, enjoyed by locals and visitors alike as a testament to the country's rich culinary heritage and its deep connection to the sea.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound mixed seafood (clams, mussels, shrimp, squid)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 4 cups fish or seafood stock
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges

Recipe

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until softened.
  2. Add the paprika, cumin, oregano, and cayenne pepper (if using) to the pot and stir to combine.
  3. Add the mixed seafood and potatoes to the pot and sauté for a few minutes until the seafood is partially cooked.
  4. Add the white wine to the pot and let cook for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Add the fish or seafood stock to the pot and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and let simmer for about 15 minutes or until the potatoes are tender.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Garnish with chopped cilantro and lime wedges on the side.
  8. Serve hot and enjoy the flavors of the Chilean coast!
