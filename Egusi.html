Egusi

Egusi is a delicious soup that is popular in many West African countries, especially Nigeria. This soup is made from ground melon seeds, also known as egusi seeds, which give the soup a nutty flavor and a thick, creamy texture. The origins of egusi can be traced back to the Hausa people of northern Nigeria, but it has since spread throughout the region and is now a beloved dish in many African households.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup ground egusi seeds
  • 2 cups chicken or beef broth
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper, seeded and chopped
  • 2 cups chopped spinach or bitterleaf
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup palm oil

Recipe

  1. Heat the palm oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic and sauté until the onions are translucent, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the tomatoes and scotch bonnet pepper and cook for another 5 minutes.
  3. Add the egusi seeds and stir to combine with the onion and tomato mixture. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly.
  4. Add the broth and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Add the spinach or bitterleaf and cook for another 5 minutes.
  6. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  7. Serve the Egusi Soup hot with fufu, eba or pounded yam and enjoy!
