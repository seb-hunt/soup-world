Borscht

Borscht is a classic Ukrainian soup that has become a staple of Eastern European cuisine. With its vibrant color and earthy flavor, borscht is a perfect representation of the rich agricultural traditions of Ukraine. The soup is typically made with beets, potatoes, cabbage, and other vegetables, along with a hearty helping of beef or pork, and is often served with a dollop of sour cream on top. Borscht is a dish that has been passed down through generations of Ukrainian families, each adding their own unique twist to the recipe.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound beef or pork, cubed
  • 6 cups beef or vegetable stock
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 large beets, peeled and chopped
  • 2 large potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1 carrot, peeled and chopped
  • 1/4 head cabbage, chopped
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh dill
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Sour cream, for serving (optional)

Recipe

  1. In a large pot, brown the beef or pork over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the onion and garlic to the pot and sauté until softened.
  3. Add the beets, potatoes, carrot, and cabbage to the pot and stir to combine.
  4. Add the diced tomatoes, bay leaf, sugar, and stock to the pot and bring to a boil.
  5. Reduce heat and let simmer for 30-40 minutes or until the vegetables are tender and the meat is fully cooked.
  6. Add the vinegar and fresh dill to the pot and stir to combine.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Remove the bay leaf and serve hot, garnished with a dollop of sour cream if desired.
