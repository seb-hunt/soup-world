Clam Chowder

New England clam chowder is a creamy, hearty soup that has been warming the hearts and souls of New Englanders for centuries. Legend has it that this soup was first made by fishermen who were looking for a way to use up their leftover catch of the day. Today, it's a classic American dish that's enjoyed across the country. The soup is made with tender chunks of potatoes, succulent clams, and crispy bacon, all swimming in a velvety broth made with milk and cream. It's the ultimate comfort food, perfect for a chilly day or any time you're in need of a warm and comforting hug in a bowl. So grab a spoon and dig in to the deliciousness that is New England clam chowder!

Ingredients

  • 6 slices of bacon, diced
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 cans of chopped clams, drained (reserve the juice)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley or chives, chopped (optional)

Recipe

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, cook the diced bacon over medium heat until crisp. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside on a paper towel-lined plate.
  2. Add the butter to the pot and let it melt. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Sprinkle the flour over the onions and garlic, and stir to combine. Cook for 1-2 minutes until the flour is lightly browned.
  4. Slowly whisk in the whole milk and reserved clam juice, and stir until smooth. Add the diced potatoes to the pot and bring the mixture to a boil.
  5. Reduce heat to low and let simmer until the potatoes are tender, about 15-20 minutes.
  6. Add the drained clams and heavy cream to the pot and stir to combine. Cook for an additional 5 minutes until heated through.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Garnish with the reserved crispy bacon and chopped parsley or chives, if desired.
  9. Serve hot and enjoy your delicious New England clam chowder!
