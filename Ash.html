Ash

Ash is a traditional Persian soup that has been enjoyed for centuries in Iran and neighboring countries. The word "Ash" means "thick soup" and there are many different variations of the dish. One of the most popular is Ash-e-reshteh, a hearty soup made with beans, noodles, and herbs. Another popular variation is Ash-e-Anar, made with pomegranate and chicken. Ash is traditionally served during the winter months to provide warmth and nourishment. The origins of Ash can be traced back to ancient Persia, where it was a staple food in the royal courts and enjoyed by people from all walks of life.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of chopped parsley
  • 1 cup of chopped cilantro
  • 1 cup of chopped scallions
  • 1 cup of chopped spinach
  • 1 cup of lentils
  • 1/2 cup of basmati rice
  • 1/2 cup of chickpeas
  • 1/2 cup of dried apricots, chopped
  • 1/2 cup of dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup of walnuts, chopped
  • 1/2 cup of raisins
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of turmeric
  • 1 tablespoon of cumin
  • 1 tablespoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of black pepper
  • 6 cups of water
  • 1 lemon, juiced

Recipe

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Add the scallions and sauté for 2-3 minutes, until softened.
  3. Add the parsley, cilantro, spinach, and lentils to the pot and stir to combine.
  4. Add the basmati rice, chickpeas, apricots, cranberries, walnuts, turmeric, cumin, salt, and black pepper to the pot and stir to combine.
  5. Pour in the water and bring the soup to a boil.
  6. Reduce the heat to low and let the soup simmer for 45-50 minutes, until the lentils and rice are tender.
  7. Add the lemon juice to the pot and stir to combine.
  8. Serve the soup hot and enjoy!
