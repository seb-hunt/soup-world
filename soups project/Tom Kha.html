Tom Kha

Tom kha is a delicious and fragrant Thai soup that is bursting with flavor. The soup is made with coconut milk, lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves, and other herbs and spices. It has a creamy, tangy, and slightly spicy taste that is sure to tantalize your taste buds. Tom kha is believed to have originated in central Thailand, where it was served to the royal family as a delicacy. Today, it is enjoyed all over the world and is a popular menu item in Thai restaurants. Whether you're looking for something warm and comforting on a cold day, or you simply want to try something new and exciting, Tom kha is the perfect choice!

Ingredients

  • 1 can of coconut milk
  • 2 cups of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 stalk of lemongrass, sliced thinly
  • 1 inch of galangal, sliced
  • 3-4 kaffir lime leaves
  • 1 tablespoon of fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon of palm sugar
  • 1-2 Thai chilies, sliced thinly (optional)
  • 1/2 cup of sliced mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup of sliced chicken or tofu
  • 1/4 cup of chopped cilantro (optional)

Recipe

  1. In a large pot, combine the coconut milk and chicken or vegetable broth. Heat over medium heat until it comes to a boil.
  2. Add the lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Stir in the fish sauce, lime juice, palm sugar, and Thai chilies (if using). Add the mushrooms and chicken/tofu and simmer for another 5-7 minutes until the chicken is cooked and the mushrooms are tender.
  4. Remove the pot from the heat and let it cool slightly. Remove the lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves.
  5. Garnish with cilantro, if desired. Serve hot and enjoy!
