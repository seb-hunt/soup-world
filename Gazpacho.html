Gazpacho

Gazpacho is a refreshing, chilled soup that originated in the southern region of Spain, Andalusia. Traditionally made with a variety of fresh vegetables, such as tomatoes, cucumbers, and bell peppers, it is the perfect dish for hot summer days. Gazpacho is usually served cold and can be a great appetizer or even a light meal. This classic Spanish soup is a delicious way to beat the heat while enjoying the vibrant flavors of the Mediterranean.

Ingredients

  • 6 large ripe tomatoes, roughly chopped
  • 1 medium cucumber, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 1 medium green bell pepper, roughly chopped
  • 1 small red onion, roughly chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons sherry vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 cups cold water
  • Optional garnish: diced tomato, cucumber, red onion, croutons, or fresh herbs

Recipe

  1. In a blender or food processor, combine the chopped tomatoes, cucumber, bell pepper, red onion, and minced garlic. Blend until the mixture is smooth.
  2. Add the olive oil, sherry vinegar, salt, and black pepper to the blender and blend again to combine.
  3. With the blender running, slowly add the cold water until the gazpacho reaches the desired consistency. You may need to add more or less water depending on the thickness of the soup.
  4. Transfer the gazpacho to a large bowl and chill in the refrigerator for at least an hour before serving.
  5. Garnish with diced tomato, cucumber, red onion, croutons, or fresh herbs as desired.
  6. Serve cold and enjoy!
