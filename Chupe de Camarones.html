Chupe de Camarones

Peruvian cuisine is renowned for its bold and vibrant flavors, and chupe de mariscos is no exception. This hearty seafood stew has its roots in Peru's coastal regions, where fresh seafood is abundant and the culinary traditions are rich and diverse. Chupe de mariscos is made with a variety of seafood, including shrimp, squid, and clams, as well as potatoes, corn, and a medley of herbs and spices. It's a dish that's steeped in history, with origins that can be traced back to the pre-Columbian era, when the Inca people relied on the bounty of the sea to sustain their communities. Today, chupe de mariscos is enjoyed throughout Peru and beyond, as a testament to the country's rich culinary heritage and its deep connection to the sea. So if you're looking for a taste of Peru, grab a spoon and savor the bold and vibrant flavors of this beloved dish!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound mixed seafood (shrimp, squid, clams)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 4 cups fish or seafood stock
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen corn kernels
  • 1 cup evaporated milk
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges

Recipe

  1. Heat the oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until softened.
  2. Add the cumin, oregano, paprika, and turmeric to the pot and stir to combine.
  3. Add the mixed seafood to the pot and sauté until cooked through, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the white wine to the pot and let cook for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Add the fish or seafood stock, potatoes, and corn to the pot and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and let simmer for about 20 minutes or until the potatoes are tender.
  6. In a small bowl, whisk together the evaporated milk and cornstarch until smooth. Add the mixture to the pot and stir to combine. Let simmer for an additional 5 minutes or until the soup has thickened.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot, garnished with chopped cilantro and lime wedges on the side.
  9. Buen provecho! (Enjoy your meal!)
