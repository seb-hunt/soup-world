Seolleongtang

Seolleongtang is a delicious and comforting soup that has been a staple in Korean cuisine for centuries. It's made with beef bones that are simmered for hours until the broth becomes milky-white and rich in flavor. Legend has it that the soup was first created by Korean farmers who wanted to make the most of every part of the cow. They would boil the bones and scraps of meat for hours until they had a nourishing and hearty soup that would keep them going all day. Today, Seolleongtang is enjoyed all over Korea and is especially popular during the cold winter months. It's often served with rice, kimchi, and other side dishes, making it a truly satisfying and filling meal.

Ingredients

  • 4-5 pounds beef bones (preferably leg bones)
  • 12 cups water
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 6 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2-3 green onions, sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Recipe

  1. Put the beef bones in a large pot and cover them with water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then lower the heat and simmer for 4-6 hours. Skim off any foam that rises to the top.
  2. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and green onions to the pot and simmer for another 1-2 hours, until the broth becomes milky-white and rich in flavor.
  3. Remove the bones and strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve. Return the broth to the pot and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Simmer the broth for another 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  5. Serve the Seolleongtang hot, with rice, kimchi, and other side dishes if desired.
