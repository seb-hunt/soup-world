Pho

Pho is a delicious and aromatic noodle soup that originated in Vietnam in the early 20th century. It is said that the dish was created by Vietnamese street vendors who combined French and Vietnamese culinary influences to make a unique and flavorful soup. Pho is typically made with a beef or chicken broth that is simmered for hours with a variety of spices and aromatics such as star anise, cinnamon, and ginger. The broth is then served with rice noodles, thinly sliced meat, and fresh herbs such as cilantro and Thai basil. Pho is a staple dish in Vietnamese cuisine and has become popular all around the world for its hearty and comforting flavors. Whether you're enjoying a bowl in a busy street stall in Vietnam or at your favorite local restaurant, pho is sure to warm your heart and satisfy your taste buds!

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef bones
  • 1 onion, peeled and halved
  • 2-inch piece of ginger, sliced
  • 2 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 6 cloves
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 1 lb rice noodles
  • 1 lb beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 1 bunch scallions, thinly sliced
  • 1 bunch cilantro, chopped
  • 1 bunch Thai basil, chopped
  • 2 limes, cut into wedges
  • Sriracha and hoisin sauce, for serving

Recipe

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F. Place the beef bones, onion, and ginger on a baking sheet and roast for 30 minutes until browned.
  2. Transfer the roasted bones, onion, and ginger to a large pot and add enough water to cover. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 2-3 hours.
  3. Remove any scum that rises to the surface. Add the star anise, cinnamon stick, coriander seeds, fennel seeds, cloves, and salt. Simmer for another hour.
  4. Strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve and return it to the pot. Discard the solids.
  5. Meanwhile, cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions.
  6. Divide the cooked noodles among bowls. Top with the sliced beef.
  7. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles and beef. Serve with scallions, cilantro, Thai basil, lime wedges, Sriracha, and hoisin sauce on the side.
