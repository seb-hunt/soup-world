Mulukhiyah

Mulukhiyah is a traditional dish enjoyed in many Middle Eastern and African countries. The dish gets its name from the main ingredient, a leafy green vegetable known as Jute Mallow. The leaves are finely chopped and then cooked in a flavorful broth, resulting in a thick and satisfying soup. Mulukhiyah has a long history and has been enjoyed by people in Egypt, Sudan, Syria, Lebanon, Palestine, and many other countries for centuries. It's often served with rice or bread and is a staple food during special occasions and holidays. The origins of Mulukhiyah can be traced back to ancient Egypt, where it was considered a food fit for royalty. Today, it's still beloved by many and is considered a symbol of hospitality and generosity.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound fresh or frozen Mulukhiyah leaves
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 pound boneless chicken, cut into small pieces (optional)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • Salt to taste
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Recipe

  1. If using fresh Mulukhiyah, wash and finely chop the leaves. If using frozen, thaw the leaves and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until softened and translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and sauté for another minute.
  3. Add the boneless chicken to the pot and cook until browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. If not using chicken, skip this step.
  4. Add the Mulukhiyah leaves to the pot along with the chicken or vegetable broth. Stir to combine.
  5. Add the ground coriander, cumin, black pepper, cinnamon, cardamom, and salt to taste. Stir to combine.
  6. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the Mulukhiyah leaves are fully cooked and tender.
  7. Using an immersion blender, blend the soup until it reaches a smooth consistency. If you don't have an immersion blender, carefully transfer the soup to a blender and blend until smooth, then return to the pot.
  8. Adjust the seasoning as needed, then serve hot with lemon wedges on the side.
