Feijoada

Feijoada is a dish that's as rich in history and culture as it is in flavor. This hearty and flavorful stew has its roots in Brazil, where it's considered a national dish and a source of national pride. Feijoada is made with black beans, a variety of meats like pork, beef, and sausage, and a medley of spices and seasonings. It's a dish that's meant to be enjoyed slowly, with friends and family, and is often accompanied by rice, farofa (toasted manioc flour), and couve (sauteed collard greens). Its origins can be traced back to the era of slavery in Brazil, when slaves would use the leftover parts of the pig that their masters wouldn't eat to create this delicious and filling stew. Today, feijoada is enjoyed by people of all walks of life, and is a testament to the rich culinary heritage of Brazil. So if you're looking for a taste of Brazil, grab a bowl of feijoada, raise a glass of caipirinha, and savor the rich flavors and vibrant history of this beloved dish!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound black beans
  • 1 pound pork shoulder, cut into cubes
  • 1/2 pound beef brisket, cut into cubes
  • 1/2 pound smoked sausage, sliced
  • 1/2 pound bacon, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 5 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Farofa (toasted manioc flour)
  • Couve (sauteed collard greens)
  • Cooked white rice
  • Orange slices for serving (optional)

Recipe

  1. Soak the black beans in water overnight.
  2. In a large pot, cook the pork, beef, sausage, and bacon until browned. Remove from the pot and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, sauté the onion and garlic until softened.
  4. Add the bay leaf, cumin, paprika, and oregano to the pot and stir to combine.
  5. Add the black beans and enough water to cover them by about 2 inches. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let simmer for about 2 hours or until the beans are tender.
  6. Return the meats to the pot and let simmer for an additional 30 minutes to an hour.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot over white rice, with farofa and couve on the side.
  9. Optionally, serve with orange slices on the side for a refreshing burst of flavor.
  10. Bom apetite! (Enjoy your meal!)
